> Posts / Initial Commit: Open Bike

Repository: https://codeberg.org/cyberme0w/open-bike

For some time now, I’ve been annoyed with NextBike’s application. I do appreciate the work that went into it, however it seems to be at best a bloated application, and at its worst, amateur hour. From random pop-up toasts like ‘LOL’, to the fact that the map crawls to a halt whenever you open it, because there is no way to save your vehicle filter, it is just a pain to use. So I decided to make my own frontend.

Credit should go where credit is due, the effort behind reverse-engineering NextBike’s API goes to cybre-finn. They have published their research results as well as developed Next Companion, which is a minimal Android client implementation.

Minimal really does mean minimal - the application allows one to sign-in, rent a bike, and see what bikes are currently active on the account, but that’s it.

Personally, I’d like to have just a little bit more ‘hmph’ - for instance:

  • A locally stored history of what rides I took and how long they took
  • Re-triggering an unlock, since sometimes locks get stuck
  • A QR-Code scanner, to avoid having to manually type in the bike-ID
  • A prettier design in general, as the original is quite minimal

I am also writting the application in Kotlin instead of the minimal client’s Java, which should be fun to learn.
